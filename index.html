Find Out More

Our fraternity was founded on January 10, 1899 at Illinois Wesleyan University in Bloomington, Illinois.

Currently, our brotherhood is 109 men strong; though, we look to expand in the coming semesters. We maintain a 3.0 cumulative GPA well above the all-man's average with over 95% of our brotherhood involved in other organizations on campus including participation in the Keller Interfraternity Sports League. Additionally, we have brothers heavily involved with the Interfraternity Council (IFC), Central Planning Team (CPT) for the Rutgers University Dance Marathon, Rutgers Club Wrestling Team, Rutgers Club Roller Hockey Team, RUPA, and various speciality groups. We maintain an alumni-base of over 1,200 fraters calling for many networking opportunities. Our philanthropic endeavors include raising funds for the St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, the Alzheimer's Association, the American Cancer Society as a part of Relay for Life at Rutgers University, and Embrace Kids Foundation as a part of the Rutgers University Dance Marathon. In addition, we host mixers and philanthropy events with the university's top sororities. Aside from all this, we host weekly brotherhood events throughout the year including paintball trips at Skirmish Park, pick-up basketball games, semesterly brotherhood feasts, golf outings and softball tournaments with our alumni. To learn more about TKE and to see if it is the right fit for you come to one of our rush events during the recruitment period, check our our social media accounts, or text/email our Recruitment Chairman personally (information is below).

Why Choose TKE?

The Intangibles

Young men come to Tau Kappa Epsilon for the promise of a richer college experience, a lifelong bond of friendship and a chance to grow as leaders and men. They go on to carry the fraternity's highest values—love, charity, esteem—through principled lives and successful careers.

Our Mission

We aspire to be the leading 21st century college fraternity with a focused mission on building better men who, in turn, will build a better world.
